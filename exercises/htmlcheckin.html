How to Make the Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

These brown butter chocolate chip cookies are my tried and true cookie recipe. They have been a hit at every party and event, and you can make them too!

Recipe credit: Southern Living

Total time: 1 hour and 50 minutes

Makes about 30 cookies

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup salted butter
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 and 1/4 cup + 2 tbsp all purpose flour (to make this gluten free, I recommend substituting with King Arthur's Gluten-Free Baking Mix)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Chocolate chips (as many as your heart desires)

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a pan over medium heat, stirring constantly until the butter is foamy. The butter should be a golden brown and have little brown specks floating in it. Immediately remove butter from heat. (About 8 minutes)
  2. Cover butter and let it sit for about 10 to 15 minutes. Then chill the butter in the fridge until it begins to solidify. (About 1 hour)
  3. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  4. In a large bowl, beat chilled butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until creamy.
  5. Add eggs and vanilla to butter and sugar mixture, beating until blended.
  6. In a seperate bowl, stir together flour and baking soda. (I recommend sifting the flour beforehand to avoid lumps)
  7. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients in small increments, beating on low speed until blended.
  8. Fold in chocolate chips until evenly distributed.
  9. Place 2-inch balls of dough on a lined baking sheet. If dough is sticky, place the dough in the fridge for 5 to 10 minutes until solid again. (However, I recommend chilling your dough overnight for the perfect texture)
  10. Bake until edges of cookies are golden. (about 11-13 minutes)
  11. Transfer cookies to cooling rack and cool. (about 15 minutes)
  12. Enjoy!
